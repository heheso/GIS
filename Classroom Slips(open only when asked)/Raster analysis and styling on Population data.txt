Question: Demonstrate basic raster analysis and styling. Use population density grid data.**


Raster Data:** Unlike shapes, a raster is a continuous grid of pixels (like a digital photograph). In a "population density grid," every single pixel contains a number representing how many people live in that specific square area.


============================================================== Phase 1: Load the Raster Data =========================================================================

1. **Add the Layer:**
* *Action:* Go to **Layer** > **Add Layer** > **Add Raster Layer... > Browse to the folder, select the population dataset (e.g., `Mumbai_Population.tif`), and click **Add**.
* *Result:* A grayscale blocky image will appear on your screen.


================================================================== Phase 2: Raster Styling (Making it readable) ==========================================================

We need to apply a color gradient so the numbers make visual sense.

1. **Open Symbology:**
 - Right-click your population raster layer in the Layers Panel and select **Properties**.
 - Go to the **Symbology** tab on the left. 2.  **Apply Pseudocolor:**
 - At the very top, change the **Render type** from *Singleband gray* to **Singleband pseudocolor**.
 - Go to the **Color ramp** dropdown. Choose a logical color scheme, like **Reds** or **Magma** (where light colors mean low population, and dark colors mean high population).
 - Min: 0 && Max: 240

2. **Classify and Apply:**
 - Click the **Classify** button at the bottom. You will see values populate in the white box, mapping numbers to colors.
 - Action:* Click **OK**.
 - Result:* Your map will instantly transform into a beautiful heat map showing population clusters!


==================================================================== Phase 3: Basic Raster Analysis (The Raster Calculator) ===========================================================

Now for the "Analysis" part. Let's say we want to isolate only the highly dense areas (e.g., pixels where the population is greater than 1000).

1. Open the Raster Calculator:
 - On the very top menu bar, click **Raster** > **Raster Calculator...**
2. Set the Output:
 - Look for the **Output layer** box at the top right. Click the three dots (`...`) and save the new file as `High_Density_Areas.tif` in your practical folder.

3. **Write the Mathematical Expression:**
 - In the top left box (Raster Bands), double-click your population layer. It will drop into the large Expression box at the bottom.
 - Type:  `> 1000` right next to it.
 - "poppuaiton-data" > '1000` / 100


3. **Run the Analysis:**
Click **OK**.>  A brand new layer will appear!
